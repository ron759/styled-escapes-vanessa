Why a cruise?

One booking.
Endless destinations.

A cruise is the only vacation where you unpack once and wake up somewhere new every day. Here's what makes it one of the best values in travel — especially when you book through a travel consultant.

🛳️
Unpack Once
Your cabin is your home base. Visit multiple countries, ports, and islands — with your luggage waiting for you each night.
🍽️
All-Inclusive Value
Most meals, entertainment, and activities are included in the fare. Budgeting is simple. Surprises are rare.
🗺️
Multiple Destinations
A 7-night Caribbean cruise can hit four to six islands. Compare that to flying, booking hotels, and arranging transfers for each one.

Every major cruise line has a personality. Here's what you actually need to know about each one — not the marketing copy.

🌊
Best for families & adventure seekers
Royal Caribbean

The most recognized name in cruising — and for good reason. Royal Caribbean ships are floating resorts packed with amenities, activities, and entertainment that rival any theme park.

Mid–Premium
What it does best
  • Largest ships at sea — the Icon and Wonder class have surf simulators, zip lines, and indoor skydiving
  • Exceptional kids and teen programming — kids are genuinely entertained, not just babysat
  • Wide variety of dining options at every price point
  • Private island destinations (Perfect Day at CocoCay) exclusive to the line
  • Loyalty program (Crown & Anchor) rewards repeat cruisers well
Best for
  • Families with kids of any age — the onboard options are unmatched
  • First-time cruisers who want "a lot to do"
  • Groups with varied interests — there's something for everyone
  • Active travelers who want more than just pool and beach
  • Travelers who want a private island day built into the itinerary

Vanessa's take: Royal is my go-to recommendation for families and first-timers. The ships are impressive, the entertainment is genuinely good, and CocoCay is one of the best days you'll have on any vacation. Book early — the best cabins and dining reservations go fast.

Best for budget travelers & fun seekers
Carnival Cruise Line

America's most popular cruise line. Carnival delivers a lively, casual, fun-first experience at a price point that's hard to beat. This is the "Fun Ship" promise — and they mean it.

Budget–Mid
What it does best
  • Best value in cruising — consistently the most affordable major line
  • Lively atmosphere — great for groups, bachelorettes, and people who want to socialize
  • Guy's Burger Joint and BlueIguana Cantina are genuinely excellent free dining options
  • Dr. Seuss Bookville and WaterWorks are great for families with younger kids
  • Short itineraries (3–5 nights) make it accessible for first-timers testing the waters
Best for
  • Budget-conscious travelers who want a real vacation without the premium price
  • Friend groups and bachelorette/bachelor trips
  • Younger travelers and first-timers
  • Families with young children
  • Anyone who wants a rowdy, social, party-friendly atmosphere

Vanessa's take: Carnival gets a bad reputation from people who haven't been on it in a decade. The newer ships are really impressive. If budget is a real concern and you want a legitimate cruise experience — not a compromise — Carnival delivers. Great for groups who want to have fun and not spend a fortune doing it.

Best for flexibility & freestyle dining
Norwegian Cruise Line

Norwegian invented "freestyle cruising" — and it changed the industry. No fixed dining times, no formal nights unless you want them, and consistently strong specialty restaurant options across the fleet.

Mid–Premium
What it does best
  • Freestyle dining — eat when you want, where you want, no reservations required for main dining
  • The Haven — Norwegian's luxury ship-within-a-ship concept with exclusive dining, sundeck, and concierge
  • Strong Broadway-caliber entertainment (Jersey Boys, Six, Kinky Boots on various ships)
  • Great Alaska and Europe itineraries — one of the better lines for those destinations
  • Good "Free at Sea" promotions that bundle drinks, dining, and WiFi
Best for
  • Couples who want flexibility without the formality
  • Theater and entertainment lovers
  • Travelers who want a taste of luxury (the Haven) without full-ship luxury pricing
  • Alaska itineraries — Norwegian's Alaska product is excellent
  • Multigenerational families with varied schedules

Vanessa's take: If you've done a cruise before and want something a step up — more dining variety, better entertainment, and more flexibility — Norwegian is a great next move. The Haven is worth asking me about if budget allows; it's a completely different experience within the same ship.

Best for adults & elevated experience
Celebrity Cruises

Celebrity sits in the sweet spot between mainstream and luxury. Stunning modern ships, genuinely excellent food, a more refined crowd, and itineraries that go deeper into ports rather than just scratching the surface.

Premium
What it does best
  • Food quality that consistently outperforms the mainstream lines — the main dining room is actually good
  • Beautiful, modern ship design — the Edge class ships are genuinely stunning
  • Excellent Europe and Mediterranean itineraries with longer port times
  • The Retreat — suite-class experience with exclusive restaurant, lounge, and sundeck
  • More adult, quieter atmosphere — not a party ship
Best for
  • Couples and adults who want a more elevated, calmer atmosphere
  • Foodies — the dining program on Celebrity is legitimately good
  • Europe and Mediterranean travel — Celebrity does this exceptionally well
  • Experienced cruisers who want to step up from mainstream lines
  • Travelers who appreciate design and aesthetics

Vanessa's take: Celebrity is one of my personal favorites for couples and adult travelers. The ships are beautiful, the food is genuinely impressive for a cruise, and the overall experience feels polished without feeling stuffy. If you want more sophistication without paying full luxury prices, this is where I'd start.

Best for European style & value
MSC Cruises

The world's largest privately-owned cruise line, headquartered in Switzerland. MSC brings a European sensibility — stylish ships, a more international crowd, and genuinely competitive pricing on their newer vessels.

Budget–Mid
What it does best
  • Outstanding price-to-ship-quality ratio — the newest ships are gorgeous and very affordable
  • MSC Yacht Club — a true luxury ship-within-a-ship at competitive rates
  • Mediterranean itineraries run year-round and are some of the most comprehensive available
  • Very family-friendly — Doritos Playground and Lego partnership on family-class ships
  • International atmosphere — great for travelers who want a more worldly crowd
Best for
  • Budget-savvy travelers who don't want to sacrifice a beautiful ship
  • Europe and Mediterranean cruises
  • Travelers who want a luxury experience (Yacht Club) at a lower price than traditional luxury lines
  • Families and multigenerational groups
  • Anyone curious about a different, more international cruising culture

Vanessa's take: MSC is genuinely underrated among American cruisers. The newer ships are stunning, and if you're considering Europe, their Mediterranean itineraries are hard to beat for coverage and price. The Yacht Club is a hidden gem — it's the best luxury-value secret in cruising right now.

Best for river cruises & cultural travel
Viking Cruises

Viking is in a category of its own. Adults-only, no casinos, no kids, no formal nights — just extraordinary destinations, included shore excursions, and a ship that feels like a floating boutique hotel. This is travel for people who want to actually learn something.

Luxury
What it does best
  • All shore excursions included — no à la carte surprise bills at every port
  • River cruises through Europe are genuinely unmatched — Viking owns this category
  • Adults-only, no casino, no production shows — intentionally calm and refined
  • Exceptional cultural programming — lectures, cooking demos, local expert guides
  • Ocean cruises now rival the river product — the ships are beautiful and purpose-built
Best for
  • Adults 50+ who want a sophisticated, cultural travel experience
  • Europe river cruises — the Rhine, Danube, and Seine itineraries are exceptional
  • Travelers who want everything truly included — no nickel-and-diming
  • Couples celebrating milestone anniversaries or retirements
  • Anyone who has "done the big ships" and wants something completely different

Vanessa's take: If you've cruised before and want the next level — and you're not bringing kids — Viking is the answer. The river cruise product especially is something you have to experience. Shore excursions included, exceptional destinations, a calm ship, and no casino noise. It's not for everyone, but for the right traveler it's life-changing.
