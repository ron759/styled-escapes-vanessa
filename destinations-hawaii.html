Why Hawaii?

A destination that
earns every cliché.

Hawaii is one of those places that actually lives up to the hype — and then some. Here's what makes it so consistently exceptional, and why so many people go back year after year.

🌺
Unlike Anywhere Else
Hawaii is the most geographically isolated landmass on Earth. The culture, the landscape, and the light are genuinely unlike any other destination in the world.
🐢
Something for Everyone
Snorkeling with sea turtles, hiking active volcanoes, helicopter tours over Na Pali, or doing nothing on Kaanapali Beach. Hawaii handles every travel style with grace.
✈️
No Passport Required
All the magic of international travel — completely different culture, stunning scenery, warm weather — without a passport or currency exchange. It's the easiest "feels like abroad" trip in travel.

Most popular for good reason
The Valley Isle
Maui

Maui consistently tops "best island" lists, and the reputation is well-earned. It has the best beaches in Hawaii, arguably the best resort corridor on the planet (Kaanapali and Wailea), and enough activity to fill two weeks without running out of things to do.

Best Overall
Iconic experiences
  • Road to Hana — one of the most scenic drives in the world, 64 miles of waterfalls and cliffs
  • Sunrise at Haleakalā crater — watching sunrise above the clouds from a 10,000-ft dormant volcano
  • Snorkeling Molokini Crater — a partially submerged volcanic caldera teeming with tropical fish
  • Whale watching (December–April) — Maui is the world's best spot for humpback whale watching
Best for
  • Couples and honeymoons — Wailea is the most romantic resort area in Hawaii
  • Families — calm beaches on the leeward side, great resort amenities, easy logistics
  • First-time Hawaii visitors — the best "all-in-one" island experience
  • Foodies — Maui's restaurant scene is genuinely world-class
Good to know
  • Most popular = most expensive. Book accommodations early
  • West Maui (Kaanapali/Lahaina) vs. South Maui (Wailea) are very different vibes — ask Vanessa which is right for you
  • A rental car is essentially required — unlike Oahu, Maui has very limited public transit
  • Lahaina was devastated by fire in 2023 — the community is rebuilding; support local businesses
Best beaches Best snorkeling Whale watching Luxury resorts Haleakalā volcano Road to Hana World-class dining

Vanessa's take: If you're going to Hawaii for the first time and want to maximize the experience — beautiful beach, amazing food, iconic scenery, and a great resort — Maui is the answer. It's the island that does everything well. For couples especially, Wailea is one of the most beautiful places on earth.

History, culture & iconic energy
The Gathering Place
Oahu

Home to Honolulu and Waikiki, Oahu is the most accessible and most visited island — and it earns its popularity. It's the place to go if you want a mix of beach, history, world-class surf, and city energy all in one place.

Best for First-Timers
Iconic experiences
  • Pearl Harbor and the USS Arizona Memorial — one of the most moving historical sites in America
  • Diamond Head crater hike — a 1.6-mile trail to panoramic views of Waikiki and Honolulu
  • North Shore surf — home of the Banzai Pipeline and some of the biggest waves on Earth
  • Waikiki Beach — the most famous beach in the world, backed by a world-class resort corridor
Best for
  • First-time visitors who want everything in one place
  • History lovers and culture travelers
  • Surfers and surf spectators
  • Travelers who want city amenities alongside the beach — great nightlife, shopping, and dining
Good to know
  • Waikiki is very walkable — public transit and rideshare work well here unlike other islands
  • More crowded and commercialized than the neighbor islands, but with reason
  • Rent a car for at least a day to see the North Shore — it's a completely different world from Waikiki
  • Book Pearl Harbor tickets well in advance — they sell out weeks ahead
Pearl Harbor Waikiki Beach North Shore surf Diamond Head Best dining scene City + beach combo Most accessible

Vanessa's take: Oahu gets unfairly dismissed by people who think "too touristy" — but that energy is part of what makes it fun. The history at Pearl Harbor is genuinely powerful, the North Shore is jaw-dropping, and Waikiki is iconic for good reason. Great first island, great add-on to a Maui or Kauai trip.

The wildest, most cinematic island
The Garden Isle
Kauai

Kauai is what people picture when they close their eyes and imagine Hawaii at its most dramatic. The Na Pali Coast cliffs, the red dirt canyons of Waimea, the endless green valleys — it looks like a movie set because it is one. Literally.

Most Dramatic Scenery
Iconic experiences
  • Na Pali Coast — 17-mile stretch of fluted green sea cliffs, accessible only by boat, helicopter, or the Kalalau Trail
  • Waimea Canyon — the "Grand Canyon of the Pacific," 14 miles long and 3,600 ft deep
  • Helicopter tour — arguably the best helicopter tour in the world; the only way to see the full interior
  • Hanalei Bay — the most beautiful bay in Hawaii, ringed by waterfalls on three sides
Best for
  • Nature lovers and hikers — the trails here are extraordinary
  • Couples who want somewhere quieter and more intimate than Maui
  • Adventurers — kayaking, zip-lining, snorkeling sea caves, Na Pali boat tours
  • Repeat Hawaii visitors ready to go beyond the obvious
Good to know
  • Kauai is the least developed of the main islands — fewer resorts, more raw nature
  • The North Shore (Hanalei) is very different from the South Shore (Poipu) — consider splitting time or choosing based on priorities
  • A rental car is an absolute necessity — there's essentially no other way to get around
  • Rainy season on the North Shore is real. Poipu/South Shore stays much drier year-round
Na Pali Coast Waimea Canyon Best helicopter tour Hanalei Bay World-class hiking Most secluded Kayaking sea caves

Vanessa's take: If you've already done Maui and want somewhere that feels truly wild and untouched, Kauai is next. The scenery is almost incomprehensible — I tell clients to budget for the helicopter tour no matter what, because seeing Na Pali from the air is something you will never forget. Quieter, more romantic, and genuinely breathtaking.

Active volcanoes & otherworldly landscapes
The Big Island
Hawaiʻi Island

The Big Island is the youngest, the largest, and the most geologically active island in the chain. You can stand on new land still being created by lava, swim with manta rays at night, and stargaze from one of the clearest skies on Earth — all in one day.

Most Unique
Iconic experiences
  • Hawaiʻi Volcanoes National Park — watch lava glow at night, walk through lava tubes, see the raw power of creation
  • Manta ray night snorkel — snorkeling or diving with giant mantas feeding on plankton under lights, off Kona
  • Mauna Kea summit stargazing — at 13,800 ft, the clearest dark skies in the Northern Hemisphere
  • Green Sand Beach (Papakolea) — one of only four green sand beaches in the world
Best for
  • Adventure and bucket-list travelers
  • Nature and science lovers — the geology here is extraordinary
  • Divers and snorkelers — Kona's underwater visibility is world-class
  • Travelers who want something genuinely different from a "typical" island trip
Good to know
  • The island is enormous — the Kona (west) and Hilo (east) sides are very different climates and experiences
  • Most resorts and beaches are on the Kona/Kohala Coast — far from the volcanoes
  • Plan at least 3 nights to see the highlights; the island rewards extra time
  • Often pairs well with Maui — fly inter-island for 45 minutes and get a completely different experience
Active volcanoes Manta ray diving Mauna Kea stargazing Green sand beach Best diving Coffee country Most diverse terrain

Vanessa's take: The Big Island is the most underrated island in Hawaii. Most people don't realize how much there is to do beyond the beach. The manta ray night snorkel is one of the most magical things I've ever done. If you want to feel like you're on another planet — and still have excellent resorts and food — the Big Island delivers every time.

Before you go

A few things
worth knowing.

Hawaii is easy to fall in love with and easy to mess up if you don't plan right. Here's what Vanessa tells every client before they book.

⏰
Book Early — Especially for Peak Season
Peak seasons (Christmas, spring break, summer) sell out months in advance. The best rooms and the best prices both go early. Give yourself 6–9 months of lead time for a peak-season trip.
🚗
Budget for a Rental Car
On most islands, a rental car is not optional — it's how you see Hawaii. Factor this into your budget from the start. Book through a travel consultant and you'll often find better rates than booking direct.
🌧️
Understand the Wet and Dry Sides
Every island has a wetter north/east side and a drier south/west side. Where you stay matters. Vanessa helps every client pick the right side of the island for their priorities.
🐢
Respect the Culture and Environment
Never touch sea turtles. Respect sacred sites. Stay on designated trails. Buy local whenever possible. Hawaii's beauty is fragile and the culture is deep — travel with intention and it gives back tenfold.
🛫
Inter-Island Flights Are Easy
45-minute flights connect the islands. A Maui + Big Island or Maui + Kauai combo trip is very doable in 10 nights. Vanessa can build the whole thing as a seamless itinerary.
💰
Hawaii is Expensive — Plan Accordingly
Nearly everything is shipped in. Food, gas, and activities cost more than the mainland. Budget realistically — and know that a travel consultant can often find value the booking sites won't show you.
