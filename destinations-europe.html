Why Europe?

The trip that changes
how you see the world.

Europe isn't just a destination — it's a perspective shift. History you can touch, food you'll spend years trying to recreate at home, and a pace of life that reminds you what living is actually for.

🏛️
Living History
Walk streets that have been walked for two thousand years. Stand in the Colosseum, cross Charles Bridge, watch the sun set over the Acropolis. History here isn't in a museum — it's the city itself.
🍷
Extraordinary Food & Wine
Every region of Europe has a distinct culinary identity — pasta in Bologna, tapas in San Sebastián, pastries in Vienna, seafood in Lisbon. Food is how Europe tells its story, and it's a remarkable one.
🚂
Multiple Countries, One Trip
A 10-day river cruise can move you through four countries. A rail pass can take you from Paris to Rome to Barcelona without an airport in sight. Europe rewards the traveler who wants more than one story.

Sun, sea, and 3,000 years of history
The Mediterranean

The Mediterranean is Europe at its most intoxicating — ancient ruins, terraced hillside villages, turquoise water, and food that will ruin you for anything else. This is the region most people picture when they close their eyes and think "Europe."

Most Popular
What draws people here
  • Italy — Rome, Florence, Venice, the Amalfi Coast, Sicily. Every city is a masterpiece
  • Greece — Athens, Santorini, Mykonos, island-hopping through the Aegean
  • Spain — Barcelona, Seville, the Balearic Islands, Costa del Sol
  • Croatia — Dubrovnik, Split, the Dalmatian Coast — one of Europe's best-kept secrets
  • Turkey — Istanbul bridges East and West in one extraordinary city
Best for
  • First-time Europe visitors who want iconic, visual, and food-forward experiences
  • Couples and honeymooners — Santorini and the Amalfi Coast are among the most romantic places on Earth
  • History and culture lovers — the concentration of ancient sites is unmatched
  • Ocean lovers who want warm, swimmable water and stunning coastlines
How to do it
  • Mediterranean cruise — the most efficient way to see multiple countries; Celebrity, Viking, and MSC do this exceptionally well
  • Land itinerary — fly into one city, train or fly between stops, out through another
  • Island hopping — Greece and Croatia both reward a slow multi-island approach by ferry
  • Best months: May–June and September–October avoid the peak summer crowds
Rome & the Colosseum Santorini sunsets Amalfi Coast Barcelona Dubrovnik Venice canals Tuscan countryside Greek island hopping

Vanessa's take: If it's your first time in Europe, the Mediterranean is where I'd start. Italy alone could fill a lifetime of trips. A Mediterranean cruise is one of the most efficient and beautiful ways I know to see multiple countries without the hassle of coordinating transport and hotels between each stop. Let me build this for you — it's one of my favorites to plan.

Europe's most elegant way to travel
European River Cruises

River cruising is fundamentally different from ocean cruising — smaller ships, intimate atmosphere, every port a UNESCO-listed town or a vineyard on a hillside. You dock in the heart of every city, step off and explore, then return to your floating hotel for dinner as the scenery drifts by.

Most Unique Experience
The great river routes
  • Danube — Budapest to Vienna to Bratislava to Passau. Imperial capitals, fairytale castles, Christmas markets in December
  • Rhine — Amsterdam to Basel through the vineyard-covered Rhine Gorge. Medieval castles at every bend
  • Seine — Paris to Normandy. The Eiffel Tower at night, Monet's garden, the D-Day beaches of Normandy
  • Douro — Porto through the wine country of Portugal's Douro Valley — terraced vineyards and quintas
  • Rhône & Saône — Lyon to Provence. Lavender fields, Roman amphitheaters, Burgundy wine country
Why river cruising is different
  • Smaller ships (100–200 passengers) mean a personal, unhurried experience — no crowds, no megaship chaos
  • Dock in town centers — walk off the ship directly into the market square
  • Shore excursions typically included — especially with Viking and AmaWaterways
  • Exceptional food — river ships consistently outperform ocean ships in dining quality
Best lines for river cruises
  • Viking River — the gold standard. Beautiful ships, superb itineraries, excellent included excursions
  • AmaWaterways — exceptional food and wine program, strong value, highly rated
  • Avalon Waterways — open-air panoramic suites on newer ships; good value
  • Uniworld — most luxurious river product; each ship decorated like a boutique hotel
Danube — Budapest to Vienna Rhine Valley castles Paris & the Seine Douro Valley wine Christmas markets Normandy beaches Provençal lavender

Vanessa's take: River cruising is where I see the most repeat clients — people who do it once and come back every year on a different route. It's the most relaxed, most beautiful, most culturally rich way to see Europe. If you've never done it, the Danube in December (Christmas markets) or the Rhine in fall (harvest season) will change your life. This is one of my absolute specialties.

Iconic cities & countryside escapes
Western Europe

France, Germany, Austria, Switzerland, the Netherlands, Portugal. Western Europe is the classic European grand tour — and every country is worth a dedicated trip. It's where the great food, the great art, and the great cities all converge.

Classic Europe
What draws people here
  • Paris — the Eiffel Tower is a cliché for a reason. The museums, food, and energy are incomparable
  • Amsterdam — canals, world-class museums (Rijksmuseum, Van Gogh), cycling culture
  • Switzerland — the Alps in every direction; Geneva, Zurich, Lucerne, Interlaken
  • Portugal — Lisbon and Porto are two of Europe's most underrated and most livable cities
  • Germany & Austria — Bavaria, the Black Forest, Vienna's coffee houses, Salzburg
Best for
  • Art and museum lovers — Paris, Amsterdam, Vienna, and Florence are in a class of their own
  • Foodies — France, Portugal, and Spain each have distinct and extraordinary culinary cultures
  • Couples who want a romantic, walkable city trip
  • Travelers who want to mix city energy with countryside escapes (Loire Valley, Bavarian Alps, Swiss villages)
How to do it
  • Multi-city rail itinerary — Paris to Amsterdam to Brussels, or Paris to Nice to Rome
  • River cruise — the Rhine and Danube are the most efficient way to see Central Europe
  • Single-country deep dive — 10 days in France or Portugal rewards slow travel
  • Best months: April–June and September–October for weather and thinner crowds
Paris Amsterdam canals Swiss Alps Lisbon & Porto Vienna & Salzburg Loire Valley châteaux Bavarian castles

Vanessa's take: Western Europe is where most first-time European trips land, and the classics exist for good reason — Paris really is that beautiful. But I love pointing clients toward Portugal, which is every bit as stunning as France or Italy at significantly lower prices. If you're flexible, I can stretch your budget much further here than almost anywhere else in Europe.

History, drama & a certain kind of magic
The British Isles

England, Scotland, Ireland, and Wales offer something no other European destination can match — the combination of the familiar and the utterly extraordinary. No language barrier, extraordinary countryside, and a literary and cultural heritage that shaped the English-speaking world.

No Language Barrier
What draws people here
  • London — one of the great world cities: history, theater, food, culture, and the royals
  • Scotland — the Highlands, castles, whisky distilleries, Edinburgh's Old Town
  • Ireland — the Wild Atlantic Way, the Cliffs of Moher, Dublin's pubs, the Ring of Kerry
  • The Cotswolds — storybook English villages that look like they haven't changed in 500 years
  • Wales — Snowdonia, medieval castles, and one of Europe's most overlooked coastlines
Best for
  • Families who want history and ease — no language issues, excellent infrastructure
  • History and literature lovers — everywhere you turn is a story you already know
  • First-time international travelers — the familiarity makes it a gentler entry point to overseas travel
  • Travelers with Irish or Scottish heritage looking to connect with their roots
How to do it
  • London base with day trips — Windsor, Bath, Stonehenge, Oxford all within 2 hours
  • England + Scotland + Ireland — a 14-day itinerary covering all three is very doable
  • British Isles cruise — a scenic way to see the coastline; several cruise lines offer this itinerary
  • Self-drive Ireland — the best way to see the countryside; roads are manageable, scenery is extraordinary
London Scottish Highlands Irish Cliffs of Moher The Cotswolds Edinburgh Castle Ring of Kerry Stonehenge

Vanessa's take: The British Isles are criminally underrated as a travel destination by Americans who think they already "know" them. Scotland especially will stop you in your tracks — the Highlands are as dramatic as anything in New Zealand or Norway. Ireland is pure magic. And London remains one of the top five cities in the world to spend a week. No matter what your ancestry is, this region has something for you.

Before you go

What Vanessa tells
every client.

Europe rewards the traveler who plans well. Here are the things that make the biggest difference.

📅
Timing Is Everything
May–June and September–October are the sweet spots — good weather, manageable crowds, lower prices than July and August. Peak summer in Santorini or Venice can be overwhelming. Shoulder season is where the real Europe lives.
🗺️
Don't Try to See Everything
A 10-day trip to Europe that tries to hit 7 cities means spending most of your time in airports and train stations. Two or three countries done slowly beats a sprint through six. Vanessa will build you an itinerary with room to breathe.
🚂
Trains Beat Planes (Usually)
City center to city center in 2–3 hours, no security theater, no checked baggage fees. The Eurostar, the TGV, and the Thalys are faster than flying between most European capitals when you factor in airport time.
🏨
Location Within the City Matters
Staying in the historic center versus a suburb is a completely different trip. In Rome, Paris, or London, being walkable to the major sights transforms the daily experience. Vanessa always puts clients in the right neighborhood.
🎟️
Book Attractions in Advance
The Vatican, the Colosseum, the Uffizi, and many other top European attractions require timed entry tickets — often weeks or months in advance. Walk-up visitors frequently can't get in at all during peak season.
💳
Notify Your Bank — and Get a Travel Card
Most European countries use the Euro; the UK uses pounds. A travel credit card with no foreign transaction fees (Chase Sapphire, Capital One Venture) saves you hundreds in fees. Notify your bank before departure to avoid card freezes.
